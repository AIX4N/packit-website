About PackIt

Every suitcase has room for more.

PackIt turns the world's spare luggage space into a delivery network — connecting people who need to send something abroad with verified travelers already heading there.

The problem

Shipping abroad is slow, rigid and expensive

Traditional carriers mean depots, customs brokers, week-long transit times and prices that sting for anything heavier than an envelope. Meanwhile, on every single flight, travelers take off with kilos of unused luggage allowance.

Our answer

People are already going your way

PackIt matches the two sides. Senders post what they need to move — route, dates, weight, size, a reward offer. Travelers post their trip and spare capacity. When a route lines up, either side proposes a match, they agree on the reward, and the item flies with a person instead of a pallet.

How a match happens

From post to delivered

Both sides see matching listings for their route and dates. A proposal locks in the item details; the price is agreed through offer and counter-offer; and once accepted, the delivery moves through five tracked stages — Proposed, Accepted, Picked up, In transit, Delivered — visible to both sender and traveler the whole way. After delivery, both sides rate each other, building the trust layer the whole community relies on.

PackIt is designed for everyday items and personal shipments. Users must always follow airline rules, customs regulations and local laws.
What we stand for

Built on trust, priced by people

Safety first

ID verification with document and selfie checks, account reviews before matching, and ratings after every delivery. Trust isn't a feature — it's the foundation.

Fair for both sides

Senders save compared to traditional shipping; travelers earn from space that would have flown empty. The reward is negotiated openly — no middleman markup.

Borderless by design

Launched internationally from day one. Wherever people fly, PackIt can move things — city to city, country to country.
